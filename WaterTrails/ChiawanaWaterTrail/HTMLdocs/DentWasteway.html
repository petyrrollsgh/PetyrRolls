Chiawana Water Trail

Dent Wasteway (RM 340)

Below Selph Landing is the Dent Rd. wasteway. This was an open partially lined canal and was recently converted to a buried pipe. In that reengineering, areas of wetlands were drained and the wasteway now empties directly into the river. The irrigation district is interested in the efficiency with which it uses water and minimizing the hazard of open canals to the public. However, the district does not take into consideration the habitat and wildlife that may have evolved with these man-made wetlands. The Dent Rd. wasteway is a good example of how wastewater could have been used to create riparian habitat, provide recreation for the local neighborhood and prevent eutrophication of the river. Irrigation water weather agriculturally or residentially related contains excess nitrates, phosphates and other chemicals that may be responsible for the toxic algae blooms recently observed in the Columbia River near the Tri-Cities.
