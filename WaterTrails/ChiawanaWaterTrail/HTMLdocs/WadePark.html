Chiawana Water Trail

Wade Park
River Miles 331.5 to 330.5

Wade Park is another linear riverside park but is underutilized because of the lack of shade and picnic tables. It is exposed to the sun and wind and except for viewing boat races or fireworks is not heavily used. Wade Park does get a lot of pedestrian and bicycle traffic confined mostly to the Sacagawea Heritage Trail. There is a high-profile dock and boat ramp on the west end. Unfortunately, the shoreline around the dock is often clogged with tumble weed or has a 2-foot erosion embankment.

Nonetheless, Wade Park is a good location to terminate a trip from Chiawana or points further upstream or for a quick stop for a potty break. There are outhouses at the extreme west and east ends. Despite the unfriendly shoreline, the Park is also a good spot for learning kayaking or paddle boarding as, like with Chiawana Park the water is slow moving and most of the boat traffic stays on the Kennewick side.

We give Wade Park a WAR-2 or WAR-3 rating because of the embankments but there is one spot on the east end that might be less problematic and is relatively close to the parking lot.

The next stopping point below Wade Park is Schlagel Park, another 2.5 miles downstream. Between these points there are dikes and industrial areas that do not provide access except in extreme circumstances.
