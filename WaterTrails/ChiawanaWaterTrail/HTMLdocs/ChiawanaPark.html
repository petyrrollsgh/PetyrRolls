Chiawana Water Trail

Chiawana Park
River Miles 335.6 to 333.5


×

Chiawana Park is a 2-mile-long riverside park with excellent access points at the west end dock, the shoreline on the east end and the boat ramp. Both ends have shaded areas, picnic tables and restrooms. Neither the west end nor the boat ramp docks are friendly to low profile watercraft. The west end dock would be an excellent site for a seasonal, low-profile dock.

Chiawana is a good place for learning kayaking or paddle boarding. The park is on the inside of the bend as it turns to the east just past Bateman Island. Thus, the water tends to have little or no current and sometimes will eddy back up stream. Motorized craft tend to use the Kennewick side as the Pasco side is relatively shallow. Indeed, the entire Pasco side of the river has considerably less motorized traffic than the Richland or Kennewick sides of the Columbia. The river narrows as it passes by Bateman Island and there can be considerable traffic in this gap at the height of boating and fishing seasons. Again, the Pasco side is shallow and out of the way.

We give Chiawana and overall WAR-2 or WAR-3 rating as the docks are high profile, the boat ramp is steep and bumpy and the shorelines are steep and brushy. We make an exception for the east end just before the inlet to the boat ramp. Here there is a gentle shoreline with not horrible access to the trail and parking lot. Hence, we give this site a WAR-1 rating.
