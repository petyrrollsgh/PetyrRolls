Tapteal Water Trail

Chamna Park Launch
River Mile 5.6

The Chamna Trailhead is a great place for hiking, biking and horseback riding. There is plenty of parking, portable toilets and shade along the river.

However, as a paddling stop it has some drawbacks. It is the furthest point one can paddle upriver. A round-trip paddle from Wye Park to Chamna and back is an excellent trip, but in fast water one may not even reach Chamna.

It is the last reasonable place to terminate a trip from starting points downstream of Wanawish dam. There are no vehicle accessible take-outs between Chamna and Wye Park. On the other hand, it can be a suitable place to start a paddle to Wye Park. The catfishing is reportedly quite good in this section of the river.

We give Chamna a WAR-2 rating as the drop to the river is moderately steep and sandy. Getting down the embankment may not be too difficult, but getting back up will require some muscular assistance because of the sand. Depending on the water level, boat entry will vary. Best to scout the launch point for the best conditions.
