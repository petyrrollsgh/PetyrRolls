Chiawana Water Trail

Ringold Springs
River Mile 354.5


×

Ringold Springs is a major put-in and take-out point for paddling the Columbia River. It is an excellent terminus for the long ~32-mile paddle along the Hanford Reach. It is also an excellent starting point for many points down stream and the paddle from Ringold to Selph Landing is about 13 miles. Paddlers will encounter few motor boats along this section. Ringold is still part of the Hanford Reach and the head of Wallula Lake is a few miles downstream. Hence water flows increases once you enter the main river and paddling back up stream is feasible only against the shoreline.

To get to the main boat ramp stay left of the canal after crossing Ringold River Rd. The boat ramp is gently sloped, compacted gravel and provides easy access. We give this a WAR 1 rating and wheelchair users will find this a friendly place to access the water.

This is a popular fishing spot during major salmon runs and can be fairly crowded. Most of the time it has only a few visitors. As a WDFW site, a Discover Pass is required. Camping is permitted and the restroom facilities are ADA accessible.
