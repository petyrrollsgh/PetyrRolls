Tapteal Water Trail

Wanawish Dam – “Danger, Will Robinson, Danger!!”
River Mile 19.3


×

Wanawish Dam is just downstream and just out of sight of the Horn Rapids Park boat ramp. Do not approach the dam closer than 200 feet, possibly 300 feet in high water. Surface currents increase at this point and it is possible to be swept over the dam. If you feel the current begin to pick-up, move quickly towards the shoreline, preferrable River Left. If concerned, as inexperience may engender, paddle carefully down River Left to the portage. If caught in the current and you cannot reach shore, paddle as fast as possible over the dam and keep paddling until free of the back current trap. Probability of success is low. This dam has drowned and will drown those who approach too closely.

The Portage

The Wanawish Dam Portage is on River Left. Click on the photo to see details on the portage. The take-out is near a large tree just downstream from the warning sign and 400 ft above the dam. The put-in is a similar distance below the dam. We give this a WAR-3 rating as the portage is sandy and long and assistance will probably be necessary.
